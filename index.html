Cindy Hartman’s passion for nutrition was ignited when taking a nutrition class in nursing school. There she learned that dietary and lifestyle changes could greatly reduce the risk of developing a chronic disease, like heart disease, type 2 diabetes, or cancer.

Cindy’s move towards positive nutrition

Cindy decided to leave nursing school because, as she puts it, "I didn't want to be part of a band-aid approach to health care, one that focuses primarily on medication and surgery, not prevention." When Medicare approved reimbursable out-patient consults with a dietitian for people diagnosed with diabetes, Cindy returned to college at the University of Hawaii - Manoa to study nutrition and become a registered dietitian. Her degree is in Food Science and Human Nutrition.

Mixing and mashing food and nutrition

Cindy has lived in Hawaii for over 25 years. She started Food for Heart: an on-site, vegetarian catering business for workshops and retreats in Hawaii. She was a Nutrition Education Coordinator at the San Antonio Food Bankand served on the American Heart Association’s Speakers Bureau while living there; taught Culinary Nutrition to culinary arts students at St. Louis Community College; consulted for assisted living, long-term, and memory care facilities; and taught the Nutrition Element component in the Ornish Lifestyle Medicine Program at Hilo Medical Center, where she also worked as a clinician.

Your Diabetes and You

Maneuvering the medical system with Diabetes

A step in the right direction for American medecine

On January 1, 2002, Medicare extended coverage to beneficiaries diagnosed with diabetes; previously, only chronic kidney disease was a reimbursable diagnosis for patients seeking Medical Nutrition Therapy with a dietitian. This approval indicated to Cindy that a baby step forward had been made by the Western medical model, a step that recognized the importance of nutrition in preventing disease. This was the catalyst for Cindy’s return to college to study nutrition and become a dietitian.

Getting care with the American Healthcare System

Medicare will cover three hours of out-patient consultation with a dietitian during the first year of being referred by your physician; two hours per year are covered by Medicare after that. Cindy’s nutrition mission is to arm you with the knowledge of how to keep your body healthy and your diabetes under control.

Coping with Inflammation using LEAP

Coping with Inflammation using LEAP

Is your food making you sick?

Inflammation has been implicated in the development of chronic diseases, like heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and cancer. It’s important to know that generic recommendations for an anti-inflammatory diet can actually CAUSE inflammation, as diet-related inflammation is specific to each one of us. Cindy has been a Certified LEAP Therapist for over 10 years. She has treated patients that suffer from a multitude of inflammatory, diet-related health issues (such as irritable bowel syndrome, migraine headaches, rheumatoid arthritis, and fibromyalgia) caused by food and food chemicals. She implements the patented Mediator Release blood test (MRT) to identify what is specifically causing YOUR symptoms, then creates an eating plan to provide lasting relief for your diet-related health issues.

Lifestyle Eating And Performance (LEAP)

LEAP is a dietary protocol based on the results of a specific blood test that can predict at 94% accuracy any given person’s food sensitivities. The blood test is called the Mediator Response Test (MRT). MRT identifies which foods or food chemicals are inflammatory for YOU. Every person’s body is different, so don’t be fooled by other people’s standards of healthy food. Even foods like broccoli, oranges, and ginger can cause inflammation.

Overcoming Inflammation

If you’re experiencing inflammation or other disease state related issues, it is very likely you will respond favorably to LEAP with a chance to completely eliminate symptoms.

Some try to cover these symptoms up, but this is never going to be a lasting treatment to any condition. On the other hand, LEAP treats the underlying cause of those symptoms.

The role that Food Allergies and hypersensitivities play

Food allergies involve your immune system and can be life-threatening, such as a peanut or shell-fish allergy. Food intolerances however, involve your digestive system, not your immune system, and can include gluten- and lactose-intolerance. It can take up to four days to develop symptoms, so these may initially be difficult to identify. With the experience of facilitating gluten-free support groups for many years, Cyndy can get you set up for recovery. She specializes in gluten intolerance, including celiac disease.
