Cindy Hartman’s passion for nutrition was sparked while taking a nutrition class at a nursing school. There she learned that dietary and lifestyle changes could greatly reduce the risk of chronic diseases (like heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and cancer)

Cindy’s move towards positive nutrition

Cindy decided to leave nursing school because, as she puts it, "I didn't want to be part of a band-aid approach to health care, one that focuses primarily on medication and surgery, not prevention." When Medicare approved reimbursable out-patient consults with a dietitian for people diagnosed with diabetes, Cindy returned to college at the University of Hawaii-Manoa to study nutrition and become a registered dietitian. Her degree is in Food Science and Human Nutrition.

Mixing and mashing food and nutrition

Cindy has lived in Hawaii for 25 years, where she has owned Food for Heart, an on-site, vegetarian catering business for workshops and retreats, for 20 years. She was a Nutrition Education Coordinator at the San Antonio Food Bank, and served on the American Heart Association’s Speaker’s Bureau while living there; taught Culinary Nutrition to culinary arts students at St. Louis Community College; and taught the Nutrition Element component in the Ornish Lifestyle Medicine Program at Hilo Medical Center, where she also worked as a clinician.

Cindy brings over 20 years of experience starting at the turning point of health care in American Society, trending towards preventative medicine. Coping with Food sensitivities using LEAP

Cindy has been a Certified LEAP Therapist for over 10 years, treating patients with food hyper-sensitivities.

What’s really going on with LEAP?

Lifestyle Eating And Performance (LEAP), is a dietary protocol based on the results of a specific blood test that can predict at 94% accuracy any given person’s food sensitivities. The blood test is called the Mediator Response Test (MRT). MRT identifies which foods or food chemicals are inflammatory for YOU. Every person’s body is different, so don’t be fooled by other people’s standards of healthy food. Even foods like broccoli, oranges, and ginger can cause inflammation.

How you and Cindy can plan for success

Using your personalized MRT results, Cindy can work with you to design a personalized food program. determining what foods and/or food chemicals are causing your symptoms.

Crafting the path to allergy recovery

If you’re experiencing inflammation or other disease state related issues, it is very likely you will respond favorably to LEAP with a chance to completely eliminate symptoms.

Commonly treated conditions with LEAP

  • Migraine headaches,
  • Irritable bowel syndrome
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Lupus
  • Psoriasis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Attention deficit
  • Hyperactivity
  • Obesity

Some try to cover these symptoms up, but this is never going to be a lasting treatment to any condition. On the other hand, LEAP treats the underlying cause of those symptoms.

What Food Allergies look like versus hypersensitivities

Food allergies involve your immune system and can be life-threatening, such as a peanut or shell-fish allergy. Food intolerances however, involve your digestive system, not your immune system, and can include gluten- and lactose-intolerance. It can take up to four days to develop symptoms, so these may initially be difficult to identify. With the experience of facilitating gluten-free support groups for many years, Cyndy can get you set up for recovery. She specializes in gluten intolerance, including celiac disease.
